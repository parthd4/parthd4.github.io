BLUElab India Project

President April '19 - Present


BLUElab India Project (BLIP) is a sustainable and socially engaged design team that develops simple technology to solve problems in rural Indian communities. Our largest project to date is a $5 low-smoke cookstove, which is now in use by over a thousand families in 10 different communities. Local women are employed to build these stoves and to teach about the detrimental impacts of indoor smoke pollution.

As president, I led a travel team to rural Gujarat to complete thermal and efficiency validation testing on our stoves in May 2019 and another team in January 2020 to strengthen our relationship with the community. I hold monthly calls with the leadership of the SETCO Foundation, our primary Indian business partner, and the Center for Socially Engaged Design at the University of Michigan.

During the school year, I manage a team of 30 people to create prototypes and secure funding. We also give talks on development at conferences and meetings, and were quarterfinalists of the Michigan Business Challenge Impact Track.
