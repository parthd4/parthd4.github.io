The Morton Arboretum | IL

Forest Ecology Student Researcher Sep '16 - May '18

I researched the phenology (timing of life cycle events) of native and invasive plant species in a forest environment. This involved weekly data collection in the field and analyzing the data through R. We compared our data to that of the National Phenology Network's database and we were able to document the phenological advantages of garlic mustard and other invasive palnts.

We found evidence that invasive forbs leaf out earlier than native forbs in forests, which is advantageous because invasive plants can then get more sunlight before the forest canopy closes in. However, invasive shrubs tended to keep their leaves longer into autumn, past when most trees have lost their leaves. This may help invasive shrubs store more energy right before winter. We presented these findings at a student research symposium and to the Arboretum groundkeepers.
